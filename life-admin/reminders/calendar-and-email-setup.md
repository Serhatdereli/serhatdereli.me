# Reminders & Email Setup

Two jobs here:
1. Get every deadline into your **calendar** so nothing escalates by surprise.
2. Have **ready-to-send drafts** for the awkward stuff (contesting a fine, asking
   a creditor for breathing room) so you don't freeze up.

---

## A. Calendar reminders

For each fine and bill, create a calendar event. Recommended pattern:

| Event | When | Alert |
|-------|------|-------|
| **[Fine REF123] discount deadline** | the discount date | **3 days before + same morning** |
| [Fine REF123] full deadline | the full date | 2 days before |
| [Bill: Energy] due | due date | 2 days before |
| **Weekly money review** | recurring, e.g. Sun 10:00 | at start |
| Monthly money review | first Sunday | at start |

**Tips**
- Always set the alert *before* the deadline with a buffer — paying same-day risks
  processing delays.
- Title fines with the **reference number** so the event is actionable at a glance.
- Make the weekly review **recurring** so it's a default, not a decision.

## B. Email / letter drafts

Fill in the `[...]` placeholders. When you're ready, I can drop any of these
straight into your **Gmail drafts** (connected) so you just review and hit send.

### B1. Informal challenge to a parking fine (council / PCN)

> **Subject:** Challenge to PCN [reference number] — [vehicle reg]
>
> Dear [Council / Parking Services],
>
> I am writing to formally challenge Penalty Charge Notice **[reference number]**,
> issued on **[date]** to vehicle **[reg]** at **[location]**.
>
> I do not believe the charge is properly due, for the following reason(s):
>
> [State your grounds clearly and factually — e.g. "The parking restriction signage
> at this location was obscured/absent, as shown in the attached photographs taken
> on [date]." / "I had a valid pay-and-display session, receipt attached." / "I
> held a valid permit, copy attached."]
>
> I attach the following evidence: [list — photos, receipts, permit, etc.].
>
> Please cancel this PCN. If you reject this challenge, please confirm the amount
> payable at the discounted rate and the date by which I may pay it, and set out
> my further appeal rights.
>
> Yours faithfully,
> [Full name] · [Address] · [Date]

### B2. Appeal to a private parking operator (private land ticket)

> **Subject:** Appeal — Parking Charge Notice [reference] — [reg]
>
> Dear [Operator],
>
> I appeal Parking Charge Notice **[reference]** issued on **[date]** at
> **[location]**. [State grounds — unclear/inadequate signage, valid stay,
> grace period, mitigating circumstances, etc.]. Evidence attached: [...].
>
> Please cancel this charge. If you reject this appeal, please issue a POPLA/IAS
> verification code so I may escalate to the independent appeals service.
>
> Regards,
> [Full name] · [Date]

### B3. Asking a creditor for breathing room (hardship)

> **Subject:** Request for payment arrangement — account [number]
>
> Dear [Company],
>
> I'm contacting you about account **[number]**. I'm currently experiencing
> financial difficulty and want to deal with this responsibly rather than miss
> payments without explanation.
>
> My realistic affordable payment at present is **[£X per month]**. I'd like to
> request [a temporary reduced payment plan / a short payment holiday / freezing
> interest and charges] while I get back on track.
>
> I'm committed to clearing what I owe and will keep you updated if my situation
> changes. Please confirm what you can offer in writing.
>
> Thank you,
> [Full name] · [Account/ref] · [Date]

### B4. Paying off / settling a smaller debt to a person

> Hi [Name], thanks for your patience. I'm getting on top of my finances and want
> to clear what I owe you (**[£X]**). I can do **[£Y now / £Z on date]** — does
> that work? Sorry it's taken a bit. — [You]
