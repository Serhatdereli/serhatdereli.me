# Start Here — the "stop the bleeding" session

**Goal:** in one 30–60 minute sitting, turn a vague sense of "I'm behind" into a
concrete, sorted list of exactly what to deal with and when. You are not solving
everything today. You are *seeing* everything today. That alone kills most of the
anxiety.

Do these in order. Don't optimise. Just go.

---

## Step 1 — Brain dump (5 min)

Open [`finances/money-snapshot.md`](./finances/money-snapshot.md) and, without
judging, write down everything you owe or are worried about that involves money.
Parking fines, bills, debts, subscriptions you forgot, money owed to friends.
Messy is fine. Get it *out of your head and onto the page.*

## Step 2 — Gather the fines (10 min)

Open [`finances/parking-fines.md`](./finances/parking-fines.md). For each parking
fine you have:

- Find the notice (paper letter, email, or the council/issuer's website).
- Write down: **reference number, issuer, amount, date issued, and the deadline.**
- Note the **discount deadline** — most parking fines are *roughly half price* if
  paid within ~14 days (UK PCNs, many US cities). Missing that date can literally
  double the cost. **This is usually your single most time-sensitive money item.**

> If you can't find a notice, that's a row too — write "find notice" as the
> action. Unknown fines don't disappear; they escalate quietly.

## Step 3 — Decide pay vs. contest (10 min)

For each fine, make one call: **pay it now**, **challenge it**, or **need to
check**. The fines file has a short guide on when challenging is worth it. When in
doubt and the discount deadline is close: paying within the discount window is
often the rational move even if you *might* have grounds to appeal — you can
sometimes still appeal after paying, and you avoid the amount doubling. (Check the
specific notice's wording.)

## Step 4 — List the other bills/debts (10 min)

Open [`finances/debts-and-bills.md`](./finances/debts-and-bills.md) and list
everything else: rent/mortgage, utilities, phone, card balances, loans, anyone
chasing you. Amount, who, and the next due date.

## Step 5 — Sort by "what gets worse soonest" (5 min)

Across all three files, you now have deadlines. Mark the **top 3 things due
soonest** (or already escalating). Those are this week's job. Everything else can
wait its turn — and now you *know* it's waiting, instead of dreading it.

## Step 6 — Set the reminders (10 min)

Open [`reminders/calendar-and-email-setup.md`](./reminders/calendar-and-email-setup.md)
and put every deadline into your calendar with a buffer (e.g. alert 3 days
before). If you want, I (Claude) can create these calendar events and draft the
contest/hardship emails for you — just give me the real dates and I'll wire them
up.

---

## When you finish this session

You will have:

- ✅ One honest picture of your money.
- ✅ Every parking fine with its deadline and a decision.
- ✅ The top 3 things to handle this week.
- ✅ Reminders so nothing escalates by surprise.

That's it. Close the laptop. You've already done the hardest part — looking.
