## Priority — which to tackle first

There are two sensible orders. Pick one and stick to it:

- **"Priority debts" first (recommended when behind):** Some debts have worse
  *consequences* than others, regardless of interest. Rent/mortgage (lose home),
  energy (disconnection), council tax, court fines, tax — these come **first**,
  even if their interest is low. Keep these current above all else.
- After priority debts are safe, attack the rest by either:
  - **Avalanche** — highest interest rate first (cheapest overall), or
  - **Snowball** — smallest balance first (fastest wins, good for momentum).

> Order of operations when money is tight:
> **1)** keep essentials/priority debts current → **2)** clear/secure the
> soonest-escalating fine → **3)** make minimums on everything else → **4)** put
> anything left toward your chosen payoff target.

## If you can't make a payment — act *before* it's late

Most creditors have hardship processes, and contacting them early almost always
beats going silent. You can ask for:

- A short payment holiday or reduced payments.
- A realistic repayment plan (offer a specific affordable amount).
- Interest/fees frozen while you catch up.
- For energy/water: hardship funds or a payment meter plan.

Ready-to-use email drafts for this are in
[`../reminders/calendar-and-email-setup.md`](../reminders/calendar-and-email-setup.md).

**Free, regulated help (UK):** National Debtline (0808 808 4000), StepChange,
Citizens Advice. They can set up a single plan across all your debts at no cost.
If you're not in the UK, search "free debt advice [your country]" — most regions
have a nonprofit equivalent; avoid anyone who charges upfront fees.
